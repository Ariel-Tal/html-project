Garlic Rosemary Herb Focaccia

Description

This homemade focaccia is soft, chewy, and full of flavor thanks to fresh rosemary, garlic, and olive oil. A simple yeast dough rises into a golden, dimpled flatbread that pairs beautifully with any meal or can be enjoyed on its own.

Ingredients

  • 2 and 3/4 cups (345g) all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon granulated sugar
  • 1 Tablespoon active dry or instant yeast
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water (about 100–110°F / 38–43°C)
  • 2 Tablespoons olive oil, plus more for greasing and topping
  • 1–2 Tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (or 1 teaspoon garlic powder)
  • Sea salt, for topping

Instructions

  1. Activate the yeast: In a large bowl, mix the warm water, sugar, and yeast. Let sit for 5 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix the dough: Add flour, salt, and olive oil. Stir until a sticky dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. You may use a stand mixer with a dough hook if preferred.
  3. First rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 to 2 hours at room temperature until doubled in size.
  4. Shape the dough: Transfer to a greased 9x13-inch pan. Use your hands to gently stretch the dough to fill the pan. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  5. Preheat the oven: Set to 400°F (204°C).
  6. Add toppings: Use your fingertips to create dimples in the dough. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle rosemary, garlic, and sea salt on top.
  7. Bake: Place in the oven for 20 to 25 minutes until golden brown. Let cool slightly before slicing and serving.

Notes

  • Make Ahead: After the first rise, you can refrigerate the dough (covered) for up to 2 days. Let it come to room temperature before shaping and baking.
  • Freezing: Baked focaccia can be frozen for up to 3 months. Reheat in the oven until warm.
  • Flavor Ideas: Add sliced olives, caramelized onions, or shredded cheese before baking for variations.
  • Fresh Herbs: If rosemary is unavailable, thyme or oregano work well too.
